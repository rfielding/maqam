# Embroidered carpet still-image source prompt

This is the source prompt/spec for regenerating the still carpet image. The generated output is not expected to be pixel-identical, but it should preserve the same visual language.

## Target

Generate a dark, ornate, embroidered carpet / textile map that visually suggests a symbolic maqam score.

The image should look like a richly woven manuscript or carpet, not a GUI diagram, not a graph, and not a panel layout.

## Prompt

A dark embroidered textile map of musical territories, like a surreal maqam carpet manuscript. Continuous organic woven regions flow into each other with no visible rectangular panels. Dense ornamental stitch detail fills every area. The regions are colored in subdued jewel tones: deep purples, greens, teals, muted golds, and dark reds, all dark enough that light terminal text could remain readable over it. Fine threads, knots, braided seams, and over-under crossings connect the regions. The seams feel like geography, not graph edges. Rhythm is suggested by repeating stripe cadence, grouped stitch bands, and nested motifs. Pitch ratios are suggested by tiny constellations of colored stitches and harmonic dot clusters. The whole image should feel like South American quipu symbolism, Islamic geometric ornament, Celtic knotwork, and a Persian carpet blended into one score-like textile. No readable text, no labels, no UI boxes, no rectangles, no staff notation, no modern infographic style. Extremely detailed, dense, dark, beautiful, mysterious, organic, woven, textile, embroidery, carpet, symbolic music notation, high resolution.

## Negative constraints

- no readable text
- no labels
- no boxes or panels
- no obvious graph nodes
- no blank background
- no white background
- no bright central glare
- no cursor
- no musical staff
- no piano roll
- no waveform
- no MIDI grid
- no flat vector infographic

## Notes

The generated still image becomes the static manuscript. Playback should not animate the carpet geometry. Playback only brightens/re-draws the current local area over the fixed image.
